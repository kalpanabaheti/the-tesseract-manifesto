manifesto

July 2026

The next era in computing will not come with smaller transistors.

For seventy years, whenever humanity reached the limits of computation, we built a better machine. Faster processors. Larger clusters. Better GPUs. Quantum computers. Photonic chips. Neuromorphic hardware.

Yet the hardest scientific problems remain stubbornly out of reach.

Not because we lack processing power.
Soon enough, we may have nuclear energy committed to that!

Because some problems cannot simply be searched faster.

The discovery of medicines is one of them.

Every year, billions of dollars and countless lives are lost searching an unimaginably vast chemical universe with almost no examples to learn from. Artificial intelligence thrives where history exists. Human biology continually asks questions that history has never answered.

No dataset can contain what humanity has never discovered.
And medicinal discovery happens to be a function of the dataset from which it learns.

We believe the missing computer has existed all along.

For decades we have asked whether machines can think like humans.
Now let us ask a different question.
What happens when millions of humans think as one machine?

The biological brain remains the most versatile reasoning machine we know.

It learns from remarkably little data. It transfers knowledge across domains. It discovers patterns no dataset has ever contained.

Now imagine a million of them reasoning collaboratively on a controlled network.

A network where human players battle in immersive game worlds.
Game worlds akin with beloved Age of Empires and Clash of Clans experiences.
Game worlds designed to be worth playing.

But with an unsolved molecular physics problem masked within it.

With a light-weight pioneered machine intelligence that coordinates the players on this search across a vast chemical space. And where every victory, though it feels like play, maps back to a solved molecular system.

A molecular system solved that once plagued suffering patients.

This is not crowdsourcing.
This is collaborative and collective intelligence at its strongest.

A computer naturally tuned for chaotic information-deficit problems.
Built on one juggernaut device, named humanity.

This is the device we’re building.
Pillared by human brains on a network.
A network of humans playing on game interfaces controlled and coordinated by algorithms

A natural superintelligence committed to the discovery of medicine.
