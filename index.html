manifesto

The next era in computing will not come with smaller transistors. For seventy years, whenever humanity reached the limits of computation, we built a better machine. Faster processors. Larger clusters. Better GPUs. Quantum computers. Photonic chips. Neuromorphic hardware. Yet the hardest scientific problems remain stubbornly out of reach. Not because we lack processing power - we’ll have nuclear energy committed to that one day! Because some problems cannot simply be searched faster. The discovery of medicines is one of them. Every year, billions of dollars and countless lives are lost searching an unimaginably vast chemical universe with almost no examples to learn from. Artificial intelligence thrives where history exists. Human biology continually asks questions that history has never answered. No data can teach what humanity has never discovered. We believe the missing computer has existed all along. For decades we have asked whether machines can think like humans. We are asking a different question. What happens when millions of humans solve as one machine? The biological brain remains the most versatile reasoning machine we know. It learns from remarkably little data. It transfers knowledge across domains. It discovers patterns no dataset has ever contained. Now imagine a million of them reasoning collaboratively on a controlled network. Human players compete in immersive game worlds. Games worlds akin with beloved Age of Empires and Clash of Clans experiences. Game worlds designed to be worth playing. But with an unsolved molecular physics problems masked within it. Machine intelligence coordinates the players. Every victory, though it feels like play, maps back to a solved molecular system. A molecular system solved that once plagued suffering patients. This is not crowdsourcing. This is collaborative and collective intelligence at its strongest. A computer naturally tuned for chaotic data-less problems. Built on one juggernaut device, named humanity. This is the device we’re building. Pillared by human players, committed to discovering medicines. Discovering small molecules for hard-to-cure cancers and orphan diseases - with intractable molecular systems behind it, and excruciatingly data-deprived. The future of superintelligence we dream of, is not artificial. It’s natural... but in a vision far exceeding our humble imagination of the human brain.
